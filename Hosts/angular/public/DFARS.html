Key Terms and Definitions

Covered Defense Information (CDI)

Covered Defense Information, or CDI, refers to unclassified information that is provided to a contractor by or on behalf of the Department of Defense in connection with a contract. This information must be marked or otherwise identified in the contract, task order, or delivery order, and is subject to safeguarding and reporting requirements outlined in DFARS 252.204-7012.

CDI includes technical data, computer software, and other information that is controlled by the government for strategic, operational, tactical, or intelligence purposes. It may involve critical technology, export-controlled information, or sensitive business information that could harm national security if disclosed or compromised. Contractors handling CDI must implement adequate security measures to protect this information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or use.

Failure to comply with CDI requirements can have serious consequences, including legal penalties, contract termination, and reputational damage. Therefore, it is essential for defense contractors to understand and adhere to the security controls, incident reporting procedures, and other obligations specified in DFARS 252.204-7012. By safeguarding CDI effectively and responding promptly to cyber incidents, contractors can fulfill their responsibilities to the Department of Defense and contribute to the overall cybersecurity resilience of the defense industrial base.

Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)

Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) refers to unclassified information that requires safeguarding or dissemination controls pursuant to laws, regulations, or government-wide policies. The protection of CUI is crucial to national security and the integrity of government operations.
